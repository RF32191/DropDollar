## OPTION 1: Business Verification Letter

**[Your Company Letterhead]**

**Date:** [Current Date]

**To:** Stripe Legal/Compliance Team  
**Re:** Business Verification - Skill-Based Gaming Platform

---

Dear Stripe Compliance Team,

We are writing to provide documentation regarding **Drop Dollar, LLC**, a skill-based gaming platform operating at drop-dollar.com. This letter confirms our business model and legal compliance framework.

### Business Overview

Drop Dollar operates as a **skill-based gaming platform** where users compete in games of skill for cash prizes. Our platform is distinct from gambling in the following ways:

1. **Skill Predominance**: All games on our platform require player skill (reaction time, accuracy, strategy) to determine outcomes. Random elements use deterministic seeding, ensuring all players in a competition face identical conditions.

2. **Legal Classification**: Our games meet the legal definition of skill-based gaming under the predominant purpose test used in most U.S. jurisdictions.

3. **Geographic Restrictions**: We actively geo-block users from states where skill-based gaming is prohibited or restricted, including: Arizona, Arkansas, Connecticut, Hawaii, Idaho, Iowa, Louisiana, Montana, Nevada, South Carolina, Tennessee, Utah, and Washington.

### Token Economy

Our platform uses a compliant virtual currency system:

- **Purchased Tokens**: Non-refundable, non-cashable credits purchased with real money (similar to arcade tokens or game credits)
- **Won Tokens**: Prize winnings that may be withdrawn to users' bank accounts

This dual-wallet system ensures clear separation between purchases and winnings, maintaining compliance with virtual currency regulations.

### Compliance Measures

We have implemented:

1. **Age Verification**: All users must be 18+ years old
2. **Identity Verification**: KYC procedures for withdrawals over $100
3. **Location Verification**: Real-time geo-blocking with VPN detection
4. **Anti-Money Laundering**: Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity detection
5. **Fair Play**: RNG seeding, anti-cheat detection, single account enforcement

### Legal Documentation

Attached to this letter:
- Terms of Service
- Privacy Policy
- Responsible Gaming Policy
- State-by-state legal analysis

### Requested Action

We request that Stripe:
1. Verify our account for continued payment processing
2. Enable Stripe Connect for prize payouts
3. Confirm our Merchant Category Code (MCC) classification

We are happy to provide any additional documentation or answer questions.

Dear Stripe Compliance Team,

This firm has been engaged to provide a legal opinion regarding the business operations of Drop Dollar, LLC ("Client") in connection with its skill-based gaming platform.

### Opinion

Based on our review of applicable law and the Client's business model, it is our opinion that:

1. **Not Gambling**: The Client's games are skill-based competitions, not gambling. The outcome of each game is determined predominantly by player skill (reaction time, accuracy, strategic decision-making) rather than chance.

2. **Deterministic RNG**: All random elements use seeded random number generation, ensuring every player in a competition faces identical game conditions. This eliminates chance as a determining factor in outcomes.

3. **Legal Compliance**: The Client's geographic restrictions, age verification, and identity verification procedures comply with applicable state and federal laws governing skill-based gaming.

4. **Virtual Currency**: The Client's token system complies with state money transmission laws through its use of Stripe as a licensed payment processor. The Client does not hold user funds directly.

5. **Not a Money Transmitter**: By using Stripe for all payment processing and payouts, the Client operates as a software platform selling digital entertainment credits, not as a money services business.

### Basis of Opinion

This opinion is based on:
- Review of Client's Terms of Service and Privacy Policy
- Analysis of game mechanics and RNG implementation
- Review of applicable state gambling and skill gaming laws
- FinCEN guidance on virtual currency
- State money transmission statutes

### Qualifications

This opinion is limited to the laws of the United States and does not address laws of other jurisdictions. This opinion is provided solely for the benefit of Stripe, Inc. in connection with its business relationship with the Client.

## OPTION 3: Stripe Application Documentation

When applying or updating your Stripe account, include:

### Business Description

```
Drop Dollar is a skill-based gaming platform where users compete in games 
of skill (reaction time games, accuracy challenges, strategy games) for 
cash prizes. 

Our games are NOT gambling - outcomes are determined by player skill, not 
chance. All random elements use deterministic seeding so all players face 
identical conditions.

We operate legally in 38 US states and actively block users from restricted 
states. We verify age (18+) and identity for all prize withdrawals.

Token System:
- Users purchase tokens ($1 = 1 token) to enter competitions
- Winners receive prize tokens that can be withdrawn as cash
- Platform takes 15% fee from prize pools
- All payments processed through Stripe

Comparable businesses: Skillz, Triumph Games, WorldWinner, Lucktastic
```

### MCC Code Request

Request classification under one of these MCC codes:
- **7994**: Video Game Arcades/Establishments
- **7999**: Recreation Services Not Elsewhere Classified
- **5816**: Digital Goods - Games

Avoid codes associated with gambling (7995).
